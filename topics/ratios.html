Hold on to those wonderings — this chapter answers most of them.

🔮 Discover — predict first

A squash mix is 1 part cordial to 4 parts water. If you pour 2 cups of cordial, how much water keeps the taste the same?

8 cups. Double the cordial, double the water — a ratio only stays the same when both parts scale by the same factor.

📚 Key words
ratio
a comparison of quantities of the same kind, e.g. 2:3
rate
a comparison of different kinds, e.g. km/h
unit rate
the amount per 1 (per hour, per kg)
📖 Read the chapter

A ratio compares quantities of the same kind (2:3); a rate compares different kinds ($/kg, km/h). Both are proportional thinking — scaling while keeping the relationship fixed.

A ratio is used to compare the sizes of two or more quantities measured in the same units. A rate compares two different kinds of quantity, like dollars per kilogram or kilometres per hour.

  • A ratio of 5 : 6 means that for every 5 of the first quantity, there are 6 of the second quantity.
  • A ratio can also be written as a fraction of the whole. In the ratio 5 : 6 there are 5 + 6 = 11 parts altogether, so the first quantity is 5⁄11 of the total and the second is 6⁄11 of the total.
  • Ratios can be simplified exactly like fractions — divide every part by the same number. e.g. 10 : 12 simplifies to 5 : 6 (divide both by 2).
  • To share an amount in a given ratio, add the parts first, divide to find the value of one part, then multiply for each share (the unitary method). For example, sharing $40 in 3 : 5 → 8 parts, $40 ÷ 8 = $5 each → $15 and $25.
  • A rate compares two different units. To find a unit rate, divide so the second quantity becomes 1: $4.50 for 500 g is 4.50 ÷ 500 = $0.009 per gram. Reducing to "per one" is what lets you compare fairly.
  • To find the better value, compare unit rates: 500 g for $4.50 is $0.009/g, while 750 g for $6.30 is 6.30 ÷ 750 = $0.0084/g — cheaper per gram, so better value even though it costs more in total.
  • Speed is the most common rate: speed = distance ÷ time. 300 km in 5 hours is 300 ÷ 5 = 60 km/h.

🍕 Pizza night

A pizza is cut into 8 equal slices. 3 have pepperoni, 5 are plain. The ratio of pepperoni to plain is 3 : 5.

That does not mean pepperoni is 3⁄5 of the pizza — there are 3 + 5 = 8 slices total, so pepperoni is 3⁄8 of the whole pizza. Ratio compares the two toppings to each other; fraction-of-whole compares one topping to the total.

🎨 Mixing paint

A mural needs blue and white paint in the ratio 2 : 3. For every 2 tins of blue, you need 3 tins of white.

Used 6 tins of blue? That's double 2, so you need double 3 = 9 tins of white to keep the same shade.

💰 Splitting chore money

Two siblings share $40 for chores in the ratio 3 : 5.

3 + 5 = 8 parts. $40 ÷ 8 = $5 per part. So they get 3 × $5 = $15 and 5 × $5 = $25.

🛒 Best value at the shop

Which is cheaper per gram: 500 g of rice for $4.50, or 750 g for $6.30?

500 g: $4.50 ÷ 500 = $0.009/g. 750 g: $6.30 ÷ 750 = $0.0084/g. The bigger bag wins — always compare the unit rate, not the total price.

Why 'find one part' works: a ratio like 2:3 splits into 5 equal parts. Know one part and every share is a multiple of it.
⚠ Watch out2 : 3 does not mean 2/3 of the total — it means 2 parts out of 5. And order matters: 2 : 3 ≠ 3 : 2.
⚡ Mental maths strategyAlways add the parts first. Sharing in 2 : 3? Say “5 parts” before anything else, find one part, then multiply up.

🕵️ Mistake detective

Diagnose the misconception, then reveal the cause and intervention.

Share $20 in the ratio 2:3 → they gave out $2 and $3.
Cause

They used the ratio numbers as the actual amounts and ignored the total.

Diagnostic question

What rule or representation would show why this answer cannot be right?

Intervention

2 + 3 = 5 parts. Each part = 20 ÷ 5 = $4, so the shares are $8 and $12.

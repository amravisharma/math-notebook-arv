🔮 Discover — predict first

Which numbers divide 12 exactly? Which divide 18? What is the biggest number in both lists?

12: 1,2,3,4,6,12. 18: 1,2,3,6,9,18. The biggest shared factor is 6 — the HCF (Highest Common Factor).

📖 Read the chapter

Factors divide in exactly; multiples appear in the times-table; a prime has exactly two factors. These ideas power fraction simplifying, HCF and LCM.

A factor divides a whole number exactly; a multiple is what you land on counting up. A prime has exactly two factors, and every whole number bigger than 1 breaks down into primes in only one way — its unique "atomic" fingerprint.

From prime factorsHCF = primes in BOTH
LCM = highest power of EACH
HCF × LCM = the two numbers multiplied

Writing a number as a product of primes turns HCF and LCM into simple bookkeeping. The HCF is the largest chunk two numbers share; the LCM is the first place their times-tables meet — which is why it drives "when do events line up again" problems.

Why: the HCF is the biggest number that fits into both; the LCM is the first multiple both share. Breaking numbers into prime building blocks makes both easy to find.
⚠ Watch out1 is not prime (it has only one factor), and 2 is the only even prime. Every other even number has 2 as a factor.
📚 Key words
factor

multiple

prime
a number with exactly two factors: 1 and itself
HCF
Highest Common Factor of two numbers
LCM
Lowest Common Multiple of two numbers

🕵️ Mistake detective

Diagnose the misconception, then reveal the cause and intervention.

They said 1 is a prime number.
Cause

A prime needs exactly two different factors. 1 has only one factor — itself.

Diagnostic question

What rule or representation would show why this answer cannot be right?

Intervention

1 is neither prime nor composite. The smallest prime is 2.

🌍 In the real world

📖 Read the chapter

Angles measure turn. A few facts unlock most problems: on a straight line they add to 180°, around a point to 360°, and in a triangle to 180°.

An angle measures the amount of rotation (turn) between two rays, in degrees.

  • Two angles are complementary if they add to 90°; they are supplementary if they add to 180°. For example, 35° and 55° are complementary; 35° and 145° are supplementary.
  • Angles on a straight line always add to 180°; angles all the way around a point always add to 360°. For example, if one angle on a straight line is 120°, the other is 180 − 120 = 60°.
  • The angles inside any triangle always add to 180°. For example, a triangle with angles 90° and 60° has a third angle of 180 − 90 − 60 = 30°.
  • When a line crosses a pair of parallel lines: corresponding ("F") angles are equal, alternate ("Z") angles are equal, and co-interior ("C") angles add to 180°. For example, if one angle is 70°, its corresponding and alternate angles are also 70°, while the co-interior angle is 180 − 70 = 110°.
  • An angle's size depends only on how much it has turned — not on how long its two arms are drawn. A tiny angle with long arms and a big angle with short arms can look confusingly similar; always check the gap between the arms, not their length. For example, a 30° angle is 30° whether its arms are drawn 1 cm or 10 cm long.

🦺 A skate ramp

A ramp meets flat ground at 25°. Since angles on a straight line add to 180°, the angle on the other side is 180° − 25° = 155°.

🏠 A roof's pitch

A symmetric roof's two slopes meet at 110° at the peak. Each slope meets the flat ceiling at (180° − 110°) ÷ 2 = 35°, using the triangle angle-sum rule.

🧭 A ship's bearing

A bearing is an angle measured clockwise from north, always written as 3 digits (e.g. 060°). A ship sails on a bearing of 60°, then turns to a bearing of 150° — a turn of 150° − 60° = 90°, a quarter-turn clockwise.

Why 180° in a triangle? Tear off the corners and lay them along a straight edge — they always form a straight line. Parallel-line angles are equal because the slopes never change.
⚠ Watch outComplementary angles add to 90°; supplementary add to 180°. Mixing these two up is the most common angle error.
⚡ Mental maths strategyAlmost everything is 180 or 360: straight line 180°, triangle 180°, around a point 360°, quadrilateral 360°. Subtract what you already know.

🕵️ Mistake detective

Diagnose the misconception, then reveal the cause and intervention.

On a straight line, one angle is 110°, so the other is 90°.
Cause

They assumed a right angle instead of using the straight-line fact.

Diagnostic question

What rule or representation would show why this answer cannot be right?

Intervention

Angles on a line add to 180°, so the other is 180 − 110 = 70°.

Ramps, roof pitches, camera tripods and navigation bearings all depend on measuring and combining angles.

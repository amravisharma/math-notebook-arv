🔮 Discover — predict first

Draw any triangle, tear off its three corners, and lay them side by side along a straight edge. What do they make?

A straight line — 180°. The three angles of every triangle always add to 180°.

angle

📖 Read the chapter

Angles measure turn. A few facts unlock most problems: on a straight line they add to 180°, around a point to 360°, and in a triangle to 180°.

An angle measures the rotation between two rays, in degrees. Two angles are complementary if they total 90°, supplementary if they total 180°. These facts, plus a few line rules, solve almost everything.

Key factsstraight line = 180°
point = 360°
triangle = 180°
vertical angles equal

Vertical angles are equal because each is supplementary to the same neighbour. When a line crosses parallels, corresponding (F) and alternate (Z) angles are equal and co-interior (C) angles sum to 180° — and dragging a triangle's angles onto a parallel line is exactly why they add to 180°.

Why 180° in a triangle? Tear off the corners and lay them along a straight edge — they always form a straight line. Parallel-line angles are equal because the slopes never change.
⚠ Watch outComplementary angles add to 90°; supplementary add to 180°. Mixing these two up is the most common angle error.

🕵️ Mistake detective

Diagnose the misconception, then reveal the cause and intervention.

On a straight line, one angle is 110°, so the other is 90°.
Cause

They assumed a right angle instead of using the straight-line fact.

Diagnostic question

What rule or representation would show why this answer cannot be right?

Intervention

Angles on a line add to 180°, so the other is 180 − 110 = 70°.

🌍 In the real world

Ramps, roof pitches, camera tripods and navigation bearings all depend on measuring and combining angles.

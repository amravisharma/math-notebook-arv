🔮 Discover — predict first

Plot (3, 2) and (−3, 2). They share the same height but sit in different quadrants. What makes them different?

The x-signs differ: (3, 2) is in Quadrant I, (−3, 2) in Quadrant II. The pair of signs (x, y) fixes the quadrant.

📖 Read the chapter

The Cartesian plane locates points with (x, y). A straight-line graph like y = 2x + 1 shows every point that makes the equation true.

The coordinate plane locates every point with an ordered pair (x, y): across first (x), then up (y).

  • A straight-line graph is the picture of every (x, y) pair that makes a linear equation true — points on the line satisfy it, points off it don't. For example, (1, 3) lies on y = 2x + 1 because 2 × 1 + 1 = 3, but (1, 5) does not.
  • In y = mx + c: m is the gradient (steepness) — how much y climbs for every step of x, or rise ÷ run. For example, in y = 2x + 1 the gradient m = 2, so y climbs 2 for every 1 step across.
  • c is the y-intercept — where the line crosses the y-axis. For example, in y = 2x + 1 the intercept c = 1, so the line crosses the y-axis at (0, 1).
  • Lines with the same gradient are parallel — they climb at the same rate but cross the y-axis at different points. For example, y = 2x + 1 and y = 2x − 4 are parallel, both climbing 2 for every step across.
Line equationy = mx + c
m = gradient = rise ÷ run
c = y-intercept

To draw y = 2x − 1 by hand: pick three easy x-values, substitute each into the equation, then plot the (x, y) pairs and join them with a straight line.

📱 Comparing phone plans

Plan A costs y = 20 (a flat fee, no per-GB charge). Plan B costs y = 5g + 5. Graphing both lines shows exactly where B becomes cheaper or pricier as data use (g) grows.

🚴 A cyclist's distance-time graph

A cyclist's distance from home is graphed against time. A steeper line means faster travel (a bigger gradient); a flat, horizontal section means the cyclist has stopped.

🗺️ A treasure map

A map marks treasure at (4, 3) — 4 steps east, then 3 steps north from the corner. Every map, game and GPS app is really a coordinate plane in disguise.

What the numbers mean: in y = mx + c, m is the gradient (steepness = rise ÷ run) and c is where it crosses the y-axis. Those two numbers draw the whole line.
⚠ Watch outGradient is rise ÷ run (Δy ÷ Δx), not run ÷ rise. A vertical line has an undefined gradient, not zero.
⚡ Mental maths strategyRead the gradient as “for every 1 step right, m steps up”. Plot the intercept c first, then walk the gradient.

🕵️ Mistake detective

Diagnose the misconception, then reveal the cause and intervention.

They plotted (3, 2) by going up 3, then right 2.
Cause

Coordinates are (x, y): the x comes first — across before up.

Diagnostic question

What rule or representation would show why this answer cannot be right?

Intervention

Go right 3 (x), then up 2 (y). Always read across before up.

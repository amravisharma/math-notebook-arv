📖 Read the chapter

Volume is the space inside a 3-D solid (cubic units). Surface area is the total area of all faces. For a cuboid, volume = length × width × height.

Volume measures how much space a 3-D solid fills, in cubic units; surface area is the total area of all its flat faces.

  • For any prism (including a cuboid), volume = base area × height — how many unit cubes tile the base, times how many layers stack up.
  • Surface area is the material needed to wrap or cover a solid completely — the sum of every face's area. Unfold a box flat and you get its net: every face laid out where you can see and add up all six at once.
  • A cuboid's 6 faces come in 3 matching pairs (top&bottom, front&back, left&right) — find one area from each pair, add the three, then double the total.
  • Scaling a 3-D shape affects volume differently to area: double every edge of a cube and its volume becomes 8 times bigger (2³), not 2 times.

1 litre = 1000 cm³
🐟 Filling a fish tank

A tank is 60 × 30 × 40 cm: volume = 72,000 cm³. Since 1000 cm³ = 1 litre, that's 72 litres of water to fill it.

📦 A shipping box

A box is 50 × 40 × 30 cm (60,000 cm³). Doubling every dimension to 100 × 80 × 60 cm doesn't just double the volume — it makes it 8 times bigger (480,000 cm³).

🎁 Wrapping a gift

Wrapping paper needed is the box's surface area (every face added up); how much it can hold is its volume — two different questions about the very same box.

📦 Unfolding a cereal box (4 × 3 × 2 cm)

Top & bottom: 4×3 = 12 cm² each. Front & back: 4×2 = 8 cm² each. Left & right: 3×2 = 6 cm² each.

SA = 2×(12 + 8 + 6) = 2×26 = 52 cm² — the flattened net above shows exactly these 6 pieces.

Why cubic units? Volume counts unit cubes filling the solid — base area × layers high. Surface area sums the flat faces, the material to wrap it.
⚠ Watch outVolume is cubic units, surface area is square units. Doubling a cube’s side multiplies its volume by 8 (2³), not 2.
⚡ Mental maths strategyCount one layer, then stack: a 3 × 4 base is 12 cubes, so 5 layers make 60. Base × height, always.

🕵️ Mistake detective

Diagnose the misconception, then reveal the cause and intervention.

Volume of a 2×3×4 box = 2 + 3 + 4 = 9.
Cause

They added the dimensions instead of multiplying them.

Diagnostic question

What rule or representation would show why this answer cannot be right?

Intervention

Volume = 2 × 3 × 4 = 24 cubic units.

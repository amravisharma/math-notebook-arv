📖 Read the chapter

A fraction like 34 means '3 of 4 equal parts'. Multiplying or dividing top and bottom by the same number gives an equal fraction — how we simplify, compare, add and subtract.

A fraction is a number, not just a slice of pizza — it marks an exact point on the number line and also means "numerator ÷ denominator".

  • The denominator names the size of each equal part; the numerator counts how many of those parts you have. For example, in ¾ the denominator 4 says the parts are quarters, and the numerator 3 counts three of them.
  • Multiplying or dividing the top and bottom by the same number gives an equivalent fraction — the same point on the number line, with a new name. For example, ½ = 2⁄4 = 3⁄6, all the same amount.
  • You can only add or subtract fractions once the parts are the same size — that means finding a common denominator first. For example, ½ + ⅓: rewrite both over 6 → &frac36; + &frac26; = ⅚.
  • Dividing by a fraction asks "how many of these fit?" — so 2 ÷ ¼ asks "how many quarters fit into 2?" The shortcut is to flip the second fraction (its reciprocal — the fraction turned upside down) and multiply instead.
  • Multiplying does not always make things bigger. ½ × ⅓ = ⅙, which is smaller than both — multiplying by a fraction less than 1 shrinks the amount, the opposite of multiplying by a whole number.

🍕 Sharing a pizza

A pizza is cut into 4 equal slices. Eating 3 of them means you have eaten 3⁄4 of the pizza — 3 parts out of 4 equal parts.

🍰 Halving a recipe

A cookie recipe needs 3⁄4 cup of sugar. Halving the whole recipe means multiplying every fraction by 1⁄2: 3⁄4 × 1⁄2 = 3⁄8 cup.

🕐 Telling the time

"Quarter past" the hour is 1⁄4 of 60 minutes = 15 minutes. "Half past" is 1⁄2 of 60 = 30 minutes.

🥥 Scooping flour

A jar holds 2 cups of flour. Using a ¼-cup scoop, how many scoops empty it? That is exactly 2 ÷ ¼ — "how many quarters fit into 2?"

Flip and multiply: 2 × 4⁄1 = 8 scoops. Counting scoops and dividing by a fraction are the same question.

Why a common denominator? You can only add pieces of the same size. 12 + 13 needs both renamed as sixths before they can combine.
⚠ Watch outYou can’t add the denominators: ½ + ⅓ is not 2/5. Rename to a common denominator first. And “of” means multiply. And when dividing, flip the second fraction before multiplying — dividing straight across gives the wrong answer.
⚡ Mental maths strategyTo compare two fractions fast, cross-multiply: for 3⁄5 vs 5⁄8, compare 3×8 = 24 with 5×5 = 25 — so 5⁄8 is bigger.

🕵️ Mistake detective

Diagnose the misconception, then reveal the cause and intervention.

12 + 13 = 25 (they added tops and bottoms).
Cause

You can only add fractions once the denominators match — adding the bottoms is never allowed.

Diagnostic question

What rule or representation would show why this answer cannot be right?

Intervention

Rewrite over 6: 12 = 36 and 13 = 26, so the total is 56.

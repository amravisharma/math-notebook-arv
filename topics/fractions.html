🔮 Discover — predict first

Is 24 the same amount as 12? What did you do to the top and bottom to get from one to the other?

Yes — both are one half. Divide top and bottom by 2. Multiplying or dividing both by the same number never changes a fraction's value.

📖 Read the chapter

A fraction like 34 means '3 of 4 equal parts'. Multiplying or dividing top and bottom by the same number gives an equal fraction — how we simplify, compare, add and subtract.

A fraction is a number, not just a slice of pizza — it marks an exact point on the number line and also means "numerator ÷ denominator". The denominator names the size of each equal part; the numerator counts how many you take.

The engineEquivalent: ×/÷ top & bottom alike
Add: same-size parts first
Multiply: across
Divide: × the reciprocal

Cutting every part into the same number of smaller parts gives an equivalent fraction — same point, new name. You can only add or subtract once the parts are the same size (a common denominator). Dividing by ½ asks "how many halves fit?", which is why you flip and multiply.

Why a common denominator? You can only add pieces of the same size. 12 + 13 needs both renamed as sixths before they can combine.
⚠ Watch outYou can’t add the denominators: ½ + ⅓ is not 2/5. Rename to a common denominator first. And “of” means multiply.

🕵️ Mistake detective

Diagnose the misconception, then reveal the cause and intervention.

12 + 13 = 25 (they added tops and bottoms).
Cause

You can only add fractions once the denominators match — adding the bottoms is never allowed.

Diagnostic question

What rule or representation would show why this answer cannot be right?

Intervention

Rewrite over 6: 12 = 36 and 13 = 26, so the total is 56.

🌍 In the real world

Recipes (¾ cup), spanners (⅜ inch) and time (¼ hour) all use fractions. Halving a recipe is just multiplying every fraction by ½.

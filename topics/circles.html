🔮 Discover — predict first

Measure around any circular lid and across it. Divide 'around' by 'across'. What number do you always get, whatever the size?

About 3.14 — that's π. Circumference ÷ diameter is the same for every circle in the universe.

📖 Read the chapter

Every circle links its radius to two measures: circumference C = πd (distance around) and area A = πr² (space inside). π ≈ 3.14 is the same ratio for every circle.

A circle is the set of points a fixed distance — the radius — from a centre. The diameter is twice the radius. The magic number is π, the ratio of circumference to diameter, which is the same for every circle.

FormulasC = πd = 2πr
A = πr²

Because π never changes, one constant scales any circle to its distance-around and its area. The area formula πr² can be pictured by slicing the circle into thin wedges and rearranging them into a near-rectangle of height r and length half the circumference.

Why π everywhere? π is the fixed ratio of any circle's circumference to its diameter. Because it's constant, one number scales every circle.
⚠ Watch outArea uses r² and circumference uses r once — and check whether you’re given the radius or the diameter before substituting.

🕵️ Mistake detective

Diagnose the misconception, then reveal the cause and intervention.

Area of a circle with radius 5 = π × 5 × 2 = 31.4.
Cause

They used 2r (that gives circumference), not r².

Diagnostic question

What rule or representation would show why this answer cannot be right?

Intervention

Area = πr² = 3.14 × 5² = 3.14 × 25 = 78.5.

Wheel sizes, pipe capacities, running-track lanes and pizza deals all come down to circumference and area.

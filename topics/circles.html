👀 Notice & wonder

Before any rules: just look. What do you notice? What do you wonder?

Wrap string around any tin, then across its middle. The around-string is always a bit more than 3 times the across-string — every tin, every size.

You might notice…
  • The ratio never changes with the tin’s size
  • It’s always “3 and a bit”
You might wonder…
  • What exactly is that “3 and a bit” number?
  • Does it hold for a giant Ferris wheel too?

Hold on to those wonderings — this chapter answers most of them.

🔮 Discover — predict first

Measure around any circular lid and across it. Divide 'around' by 'across'. What number do you always get, whatever the size?

About 3.14 — that's π. Circumference ÷ diameter is the same for every circle in the universe.

📖 Read the chapter

Every circle links its radius to two measures: circumference C = πd (distance around) and area A = πr² (space inside). π ≈ 3.14 is the same ratio for every circle.

A circle is the set of all points a fixed distance — the radius — from a centre point. The diameter is twice the radius.

  • π (pi) is the ratio of a circle's circumference to its diameter — about 3.14 — and this ratio is exactly the same for every circle, no matter its size.
  • Circumference (distance around) = πd = 2πr.
  • Area (space inside) = πr² — always check whether a problem gives the radius or the diameter before substituting.
FormulasC = πd = 2πr
A = πr²
🚴 A bike wheel

A bike wheel has a 66 cm diameter. Every full turn rolls the bike forward one circumference: π × 66 ≈ 207 cm, just over 2 metres per turn.

🍕 Comparing pizza deals

A small pizza (20 cm across) costs $10; a large (30 cm across) costs $18. Area scales with r², so the large has (15÷10)² = 2.25× the area for only 1.8× the price — better value.

🏃 A running track's lanes

A 400 m track's lanes are curved. Runners in outer lanes start further forward, because a bigger radius means a bigger circumference (C = 2πr) to cover the same number of laps fairly.

🮁 Why A = πr²?

Slice a circle into thin wedges and lay them out alternating point-up, point-down. The zigzag strip is roughly a rectangle: height r, width half the circumference (πr) — area = πr × r = πr².

Why π everywhere? π is the fixed ratio of any circle's circumference to its diameter. Because it's constant, one number scales every circle.
⚠ Watch outArea uses r² and circumference uses r once — and check whether you’re given the radius or the diameter before substituting.

🕵️ Mistake detective

Diagnose the misconception, then reveal the cause and intervention.

Area of a circle with radius 5 = π × 5 × 2 = 31.4.
Cause

They used 2r (that gives circumference), not r².

Diagnostic question

What rule or representation would show why this answer cannot be right?

Intervention

Area = πr² = 3.14 × 5² = 3.14 × 25 = 78.5.

🔮 Discover — predict first

Split a quadrilateral into triangles by drawing one diagonal. How many triangles, and what does that make its angle sum?

Two triangles, so 2 × 180° = 360°. Every extra side adds one more triangle — that's the (n − 2) × 180° rule.

📖 Read the chapter

Shapes are classified by sides and angles. The angle-sum rules let you find missing angles in any polygon.

Triangles are named by sides (equilateral, isosceles, scalene) or by a right angle. Quadrilaterals form a family: a square is a special rectangle, which is a special parallelogram — the categories nest inside each other.

Angle sumsinterior = (n − 2) × 180°
exterior angles always total 360°

Any polygon splits into triangles from one corner, and an n-sided shape makes exactly (n − 2) of them — that triangulation is the proof of the angle-sum formula. In a regular polygon every angle is equal, so divide the total by n.

Why (n − 2) × 180°? Any polygon splits into triangles from one corner: an n-sided shape makes (n − 2) triangles, each carrying 180°.
⚠ Watch outA square is still a rectangle and still a rhombus — the shape families nest, so special cases keep their parents’ properties.

🕵️ Mistake detective

Diagnose the misconception, then reveal the cause and intervention.

Sum of a pentagon's interior angles = 5 × 180° = 900°.
Cause

They multiplied by n, but the rule uses (n − 2).

Diagnostic question

What rule or representation would show why this answer cannot be right?

Intervention

(5 − 2) × 180° = 3 × 180° = 540°.

🌍 In the real world

Floor tiling, honeycomb, nuts and bolts (hexagons) and stop signs (octagons) are all about polygon angles fitting together.

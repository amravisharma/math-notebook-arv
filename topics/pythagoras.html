🔮 Discover — predict first

Build squares on the two short sides of a 3-4-5 triangle (areas 9 and 16). Add them. What's the area of the square on the longest side?

9 + 16 = 25, and 5² = 25. The squares on the two shorter sides always add to the square on the hypotenuse.

📖 Read the chapter

In a right-angled triangle, the square of the hypotenuse equals the sum of the squares of the other two sides: a² + b² = c².

In a right-angled triangle, the side opposite the right angle — the longest side — is the hypotenuse.

  • Pythagoras' theorem: a² + b² = c², where c is the hypotenuse and a, b are the two shorter sides.
  • The converse also works: if a² + b² = c² is true for a triangle's three sides, that triangle must have a right angle — the theorem can test a triangle, not just measure one.
  • Whole-number sets like 3-4-5 and 5-12-13 satisfy the rule exactly and are worth recognising instantly.
  • The rule only works for right-angled triangles, and c must always be the longest side, opposite the right angle.
  • When an answer is not a whole number, keep the full decimal through every step and round only at the very end — rounding in the middle can shift the final answer.

🪜 Setting up a ladder

A 5 m ladder leans against a wall with its base 3 m from the wall. It reaches √(5² − 3²) = 4 m up the wall — a 3-4-5 triangle.

📺 A TV's screen size

A TV advertised as "55 inches" means the diagonal of the screen (the hypotenuse) is 55 inches — not its width or height, which form the two shorter sides.

📐 Checking a corner is square

Builders mark 3 m along one wall and 4 m along the other from a corner. If the diagonal between those marks measures exactly 5 m, the corner is a true right angle.

Why it matters: it links all three sides, so knowing two always finds the third — used in construction, navigation and screen sizes.
⚠ Watch outThe theorem works only for right-angled triangles, and c must be the hypotenuse — the longest side, opposite the right angle.

🕵️ Mistake detective

Diagnose the misconception, then reveal the cause and intervention.

Legs 3 and 4, so the hypotenuse is 3 + 4 = 7.
Cause

They added the sides instead of the squares of the sides.

Diagnostic question

What rule or representation would show why this answer cannot be right?

Intervention

c² = 3² + 4² = 9 + 16 = 25, so c = √25 = 5.

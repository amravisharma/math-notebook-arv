🔮 Discover — predict first

📖 Read the chapter

An inequality (>, <, ≥, ≤) describes a range of values, not a single one. Solving is almost identical to equations — with one crucial twist.

An inequality (>, <, ≥, ≤) describes a whole range of solutions, not a single value — a region of the number line rather than a point.

  • Solve an inequality with the same legal moves as an equation, with one exception: multiplying or dividing both sides by a negative number flips the inequality sign. For example, −2x < 6: divide both sides by −2 and flip → x > −3.
  • A strict inequality (<, >) uses an open circle on a number line — the endpoint itself is not included. For example, x < 5 has an open circle at 5, and 5 is not a solution.
  • ≤ or ≥ uses a closed, filled circle — the endpoint itself is included. For example, x ≥ 3 has a filled circle at 3, and 3 is a solution.
  • Many real limits are ranges, not single numbers: a maximum, a minimum, or an allowed band of values. For example, a lift limit w < 500 kg, or a ride that needs height h ≥ 120 cm.
  • Always check the boundary itself: with ≥ or ≤, the boundary value is a solution; with a strict > or <, it is not. For example, for x ≤ 4 the value 4 works, but for x < 4 it does not.
The exception× or ÷ by a NEGATIVE → reverse the inequality sign
🚗 A speed limit sign

A sign reads "speed ≤ 50 km/h". Any speed from 0 up to and including 50 is legal — not only exactly 50.

🎡 A rollercoaster height rule

A ride requires height ≥ 120 cm. A rider exactly 120 cm tall is allowed on; someone even 1 cm shorter is turned away.

🍿 A party snack budget

You have $150 for snacks at $6 each: 6n ≤ 150 → n ≤ 25. You can buy up to 25 items without going over budget.

The one special rule: multiplying or dividing by a negative flips the sign. On a number line, negating both sides reverses their order (2 < 5 but −2 > −5).
⚠ Watch outOnly flip the sign when you multiply or divide by a negative — not when you simply add or subtract a negative number.
⚡ Mental maths strategySolve exactly like an equation, but say aloud “flip the sign if I multiply or divide by a negative” at every step.

🕵️ Mistake detective

Diagnose the misconception, then reveal the cause and intervention.

−2x < 6 → x < −3.
Cause

They divided by −2 but kept the sign the same.

Diagnostic question

What rule or representation would show why this answer cannot be right?

Intervention

Dividing by a negative flips the sign: x > −3.

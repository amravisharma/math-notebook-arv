🔮 Discover — predict first

Start with the true statement 2 < 6. Multiply both sides by −1. Is −2 < −6 still true?

No — −2 is greater than −6. Multiplying or dividing by a negative flips the inequality sign.

📖 Read the chapter

An inequality (>, <, ≥, ≤) describes a range of values, not a single one. Solving is almost identical to equations — with one crucial twist.

An inequality (>, <, ≥, ≤) describes a whole range of solutions, not a single value — a region of the number line rather than a point. You solve it with the same legal moves as an equation, with one exception.

The exception× or ÷ by a NEGATIVE → reverse the inequality sign

Why flip? Multiplying by a negative reflects the number line, swapping order: 2 < 5 but −2 > −5. So the sign must reverse to stay true. Note the difference between strict (<, open circle) and inclusive (≤, closed circle) endpoints.

The one special rule: multiplying or dividing by a negative flips the sign. On a number line, negating both sides reverses their order (2 < 5 but −2 > −5).
⚠ Watch outOnly flip the sign when you multiply or divide by a negative — not when you simply add or subtract a negative number.

🕵️ Mistake detective

Diagnose the misconception, then reveal the cause and intervention.

−2x < 6 → x < −3.
Cause

They divided by −2 but kept the sign the same.

Diagnostic question

What rule or representation would show why this answer cannot be right?

Intervention

Dividing by a negative flips the sign: x > −3.

🌍 In the real world

Age limits, speed limits, budget caps and 'minimum order' rules are all inequalities — ranges of allowed values, not single answers.

🔮 Discover — predict first

Which is bigger, 0.7 or 0.65? Many say 0.65 because it has more digits. Line them up by place value — what do you find?

0.7 = 0.70, which beats 0.65. More digits does NOT mean a bigger decimal; compare place by place.

📖 Read the chapter

Decimals extend place value past the ones into tenths, hundredths and thousandths. They're just fractions with denominators 10, 100, 1000 in compact form.

Decimals are simply fractions whose denominators are powers of ten, so place value flows smoothly rightward into tenths, hundredths, thousandths.

  • A decimal names a precise point on the number line, just like a fraction — 0.75 is exactly the same point as 3⁄4.
  • To add or subtract decimals, line up the decimal points so you combine same-size parts: tenths with tenths, hundredths with hundredths.
  • To multiply decimals, count the total decimal places in both numbers — the answer needs that many decimal places.
  • More digits does not mean a bigger number. Compare by place value: 0.5 is bigger than 0.45, even though 0.45 has more digits.
  • To divide by a decimal, multiply both numbers by 10 (or 100…) until the divisor is a whole number — this doesn't change the answer, just makes the division easier. e.g. 3.5 ÷ 0.5: multiply both by 10 → 35 ÷ 5 = 7.

× : count decimal places
÷ : ×10 both until the divisor is whole
💵 Supermarket receipt

Milk $3.99 + Bread $4.50. Line up the points: $3.99 + $4.50 = $8.49.

Misalign the points by mistake and you could be out by whole dollars, not just cents.

🏃 Race times

Two sprinters finish in 10.58 s and 10.6 s. Since 10.6 = 10.60, and 60 hundredths is more than 58 hundredths, the second time is actually slower — despite "10.6" looking like the smaller number.

📏 Measuring a plank

A plank measures 1.75 m. Cutting off 0.40 m: line up the points, 1.75 − 0.40 = 1.35 m remains.

Why line up the points? So tenths add to tenths. When multiplying, count decimal places in both numbers to place the point in the answer.
⚠ Watch outMore digits doesn’t mean bigger: 0.5 > 0.45. Compare by place value, not by length.
⚡ Mental maths strategyUse money as your model: 0.5 is 50 cents and 0.45 is 45 cents — suddenly it’s obvious which is bigger.

🕵️ Mistake detective

Diagnose the misconception, then reveal the cause and intervention.

0.7 is smaller than 0.65, because 65 > 7.
Cause

They compared the digits as whole numbers instead of by place value.

Diagnostic question

What rule or representation would show why this answer cannot be right?

Intervention

Write 0.7 as 0.70. Now 70 hundredths > 65 hundredths, so 0.7 is bigger.

🌍 In the real world

Prices ($3.99), race times (10.58 s) and measurements (1.75 m) are decimals. Line up the points when adding money or you'll be out by cents — or dollars.

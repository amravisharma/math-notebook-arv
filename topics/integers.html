📖 Read the chapter

Integers include negatives: …−3, −2, −1, 0, 1, 2, 3… They measure things below a reference point — temperatures, debts, depths — with clear rules for +, −, ×.

Integers extend the number line to the left of zero: …−3, −2, −1, 0, 1, 2, 3…

  • Every number has an opposite the same distance from zero; its absolute value is that distance, ignoring the sign. For example, +5 and −5 are opposites, each 5 away from zero, so |−5| = 5.
  • Adding is movement along the number line: add a positive number by moving right, add a negative number by moving left. For example, −5 + 8: start at −5 and move 8 right → 3.
  • Subtracting a number means adding its opposite — that is why subtracting a negative makes a number bigger. For example, 4 − (−3) = 4 + 3 = 7.
  • When multiplying: same signs give a positive answer, different signs give a negative answer. Why does negative × negative turn positive? Follow the pattern down: 3×(−2) = −6, 2×(−2) = −4, 1×(−2) = −2, 0×(−2) = 0 — each step rises by 2, so (−1)×(−2) must be +2. Reversing a reversal returns the original direction.

🌡️ Morning temperature

The forecast says −5°C this morning, warming to 3°C by afternoon. That is a rise of 3 − (−5) = 8°C, even though one of the numbers is negative.

🏦 Bank balance

You have $100 in your account and spend $150. Your balance becomes 100 − 150 = −$50 — you are overdrawn, meaning you owe the bank money.

🌊 Below sea level

A submarine dives to 250 m below sea level, written −250 m. It then rises 100 m: −250 + 100 = −150 m — still below sea level, but closer to the surface.

Why subtracting a negative adds: taking away a debt makes you richer. Two negatives multiply positive because reversing a reversal returns the original direction.
⚠ Watch out−3² means −(3²) = −9, but (−3)² = 9. The bracket changes everything. Also, subtracting a negative adds.
⚡ Mental maths strategyRead “− (−…” as a plus: 5 − (−3) = 5 + 3 = 8. Taking away a debt leaves you richer.

🕵️ Mistake detective

Diagnose the misconception, then reveal the cause and intervention.

−3 is bigger than −1, because 3 > 1.
Cause

On the number line, −3 is further left, so it is actually smaller than −1.

Diagnostic question

What rule or representation would show why this answer cannot be right?

Intervention

The more negative a number, the smaller it is: −3 < −1.

🌍 In the real world

Bank balances (overdrafts), weather, elevation below sea level and game scores all use negative numbers.

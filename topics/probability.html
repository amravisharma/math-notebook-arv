🔮 Discover — predict first

A bag has 3 red and 1 blue marble. Grab one without looking. Out of the 4 equally likely picks, how many are red — and what fraction is that?

3 of 4, so P(red) = 3/4 = 0.75. Probability = favourable outcomes ÷ total outcomes.

📖 Read the chapter

Probability measures how likely an event is, from 0 (impossible) to 1 (certain). For equally likely outcomes it's favourable ÷ total.

Probability rates how likely an event is, from 0 (impossible) to 1 (certain). The foundation is the sample space: the full list of equally likely outcomes.

Core formulasP(event) = favourable ÷ total
P(not A) = 1 − P(A)

For combined events, listing the whole sample space keeps you honest (two coins give HH, HT, TH, TT — four outcomes, not three). Complementary counting — finding the chance of "not happening" and subtracting from 1 — often turns an "at least one" problem into an easy one.

Why list all outcomes? Probability compares outcomes you want against every outcome possible. For combined events, a sample space keeps you honest.
⚠ Watch outFavourable ÷ total only works when outcomes are equally likely. For “at least one”, complementary counting is usually easier.

🕵️ Mistake detective

Diagnose the misconception, then reveal the cause and intervention.

A fair die shows a 5 with probability 5/6.
Cause

They used the number on the face instead of counting outcomes.

Diagnostic question

What rule or representation would show why this answer cannot be right?

Intervention

There is 1 way to roll a 5 out of 6 faces, so P(5) = 1/6.

🌱 In a nutshell
  • Probability runs 0 (impossible) to 1 (certain)
  • P = favourable ÷ total outcomes
  • Can be written as a fraction, decimal or percentage
  • For combined events: AND means multiply the probabilities, OR means add them

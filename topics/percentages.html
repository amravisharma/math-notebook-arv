🔮 Discover — predict first

25% means 25 out of 100. What simple fraction is that? What decimal?

25/100 = 1/4 = 0.25. Percent, fraction and decimal are three views of one amount.

📖 Read the chapter

'Per cent' means 'out of 100'. A percentage is a fraction with denominator 100, so 25% = 25/100 = 1/4 — linking straight to fractions and decimals.

"Per cent" means per hundred — a percentage is a rate, a fraction locked to a denominator of 100, so it swaps freely with a fraction or decimal: 25% = 25/100 = 0.25.

Core moves% of amount = (% ÷ 100) × amount
change is measured against the ORIGINAL

Every percentage is taken of a particular amount — the base. That's why a 10% rise then a 10% fall doesn't return you to the start: the second 10% is of a different, larger number. Working backwards (given the part, find the whole) is just as important as working forwards.

Why 10% off twice ≠ 20% off? The second discount comes off a smaller price. A percentage is always a fraction of a particular amount.
⚠ Watch outPercentages don’t simply add across steps: a 10% rise then a 10% fall lands below the start, because each is taken of a different amount.

🕵️ Mistake detective

Diagnose the misconception, then reveal the cause and intervention.

An $80 coat with 25% off costs $25.
Cause

They found the discount but forgot to subtract it from the price.

Diagnostic question

What rule or representation would show why this answer cannot be right?

Intervention

25% of 80 = $20 off, so the coat costs 80 − 20 = $60.

🌍 In the real world

Sales, tips, GST, test marks and loan interest are all percentages. Knowing 10% (just divide by 10) lets you estimate any tip or discount in your head.

📖 Read the chapter

Statistics summarise data. The mean (add ÷ count), median (middle in order) and mode (most common) describe the centre; the range (max − min) describes spread.

Statistics summarise a whole set of data with a single number. There are three "averages", and picking the right one is itself a skill.

  • The mean is the "fair share" average: add every value, then divide by how many values there are.
  • The median is the middle value once the data is placed in order.
  • The mode is the value that appears most often.
  • The range (max − min) describes how spread out the data is, not where its centre lies.
  • The mean uses every value, so one huge outlier can drag it away from what's typical — the median ignores outliers and is often a fairer summary.
  • The mean does not have to be one of the actual data values. For 2, 3, 3, 10 the mean is 4.5 — a number that appears nowhere in the list.
  • With an even number of values, there is no single middle one — the median is the average of the two middle values once the data is in order.

🏠 Average house prices

In a street of 9 houses worth about $600,000 each, one mansion sells for $5,000,000. The mean price jumps, making the street look expensive — the median (the middle house) still shows the true typical price.

🏏 A batting average

A batter scores 20, 35, 15, 100, 30. The mean (40) is pulled up by the one big 100; the median (30) better reflects a typical innings.

👞 Shoe sizes in a class

The mode (most common size) tells a shop which size to stock the most of — the mean shoe size might come out as 7.3, a size that doesn't even exist.

Why three averages? Each tells a different story. The mean uses every value but is pulled by extremes; the median ignores outliers; the mode shows what's typical.
⚠ Watch outThe mean is pulled by outliers; the median is not. One huge value can make the mean unrepresentative of the data.
⚡ Mental maths strategyMean without a calculator: guess a central value, then average the leftovers. For 48, 52, 53, 47 guess 50: differences −2, +2, +3, −3 cancel → mean 50.

🕵️ Mistake detective

Diagnose the misconception, then reveal the cause and intervention.

Median of 5, 2, 8 = 8 (they took the last number).
Cause

You must sort the data first, then take the middle.

Diagnostic question

What rule or representation would show why this answer cannot be right?

Intervention

In order: 2, 5, 8. The middle value is 5.

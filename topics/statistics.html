🔮 Discover — predict first

For 2, 3, 3, 10 the mean is 4.5, yet three of the four values are below it. Which single number wrecked the mean?

The 10 — an outlier — dragged the mean up. That's why the median (the middle value) is sometimes fairer.

📖 Read the chapter

Statistics summarise data. The mean (add ÷ count), median (middle in order) and mode (most common) describe the centre; the range (max − min) describes spread.

Statistics summarise a whole distribution of data with a single number. There are three "averages", and picking the right one is itself a skill.

Centre & spreadmean = fair share (sum ÷ count)
median = middle
mode = most common
range = max − min

The mean is the balance point — it uses every value but gets dragged by outliers. The median ignores extremes, so it's better for skewed data like house prices. The mean need not equal any actual data value, and the range describes spread, not centre.

Why three averages? Each tells a different story. The mean uses every value but is pulled by extremes; the median ignores outliers; the mode shows what's typical.
⚠ Watch outThe mean is pulled by outliers; the median is not. One huge value can make the mean unrepresentative of the data.

mean
add all values, divide by how many
median
the middle value when in order
mode
the most common value
range
largest − smallest

🕵️ Mistake detective

Diagnose the misconception, then reveal the cause and intervention.

Median of 5, 2, 8 = 8 (they took the last number).
Cause

You must sort the data first, then take the middle.

Diagnostic question

What rule or representation would show why this answer cannot be right?

Intervention

In order: 2, 5, 8. The middle value is 5.

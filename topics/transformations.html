👀 Notice & wonder

Before any rules: just look. What do you notice? What do you wonder?

Pinch-zoom a photo to exactly double its size. It looks the same — just bigger — and suddenly needs four times the screen.

You might notice…
  • The shape and proportions never changed
  • Doubling the size quadrupled the space it covers
You might wonder…
  • Why 4 times and not 2 times?
  • Which changes leave a shape completely unchanged in size?

Hold on to those wonderings — this chapter answers most of them.

🔮 Discover — predict first

Reflect (3, 2) in the x-axis. Plot both points. Which coordinate changed sign, and which stayed the same?

The y flips: (3, 2) → (3, −2). Reflecting in the x-axis negates y; reflecting in the y-axis negates x.

📖 Read the chapter

A transformation moves or resizes a shape: translation (slide), reflection (flip), rotation (turn), enlargement (scale). Coordinates change predictably.

Translations, reflections and rotations are rigid motions: they slide, flip or turn a shape while keeping every length and angle the same, so the image is congruent to the original.

  • Enlargement is different — it resizes a shape from a centre point by a scale factor k, producing a shape that is similar (same angles, proportional sides) but not the same size.
  • An enlargement multiplies every length by the scale factor k.
  • Area does not scale by k — it scales by k².
  • Double every side of a shape (scale factor 2) and its area becomes 4 times bigger (2²), not 2 times.
  • By convention, if a question does not say which way to rotate, mathematicians rotate anticlockwise — the same direction as turning a clock's hands backwards.

🎮 Video game characters

A game character flips to face left instead of right — a reflection. It slides across the screen — a translation. Its size never changes in either move, only its position or facing direction.

🖨️ Enlarging on a photocopier

A photocopier enlarges a photo by 150% (scale factor 1.5). Every length grows by 1.5×, but the paper's area grows by 1.5² = 2.25×, not 1.5×.

🧩 Wallpaper patterns

A repeating wallpaper pattern is built entirely from translations — sliding the same motif sideways and down, no resizing or flipping needed, to tile the whole wall.

Why area behaves differently: scaling lengths by k scales area by k². Double every side and the shape holds four times the space, not two.
⚠ Watch outEnlarging by scale factor 2 makes the area 4× bigger (2²), not 2×. Lengths and areas scale differently.
⚡ Mental maths strategyScale factor k: lengths ×k, areas ×k², volumes ×k³ — one, two, three dimensions.

🕵️ Mistake detective

Diagnose the misconception, then reveal the cause and intervention.

Reflecting (3, 2) in the x-axis gives (−3, 2).
Cause

They negated x, but reflecting in the x-axis flips y.

Diagnostic question

What rule or representation would show why this answer cannot be right?

Intervention

In the x-axis, y changes sign: (3, 2) → (3, −2).

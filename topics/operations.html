📚 Key words
BEDMAS
Brackets, Exponents, Division/Multiplication, Addition/Subtraction — the order of operations
operation
a calculation step: +, −, × or ÷
evaluate
work out the single value of an expression
📖 Read the chapter

When a calculation mixes +, −, ×, ÷ and brackets, everyone needs the same answer. BEDMAS fixes the order: Brackets, Exponents, Division/Multiplication (left→right), Addition/Subtraction (left→right).

Order of operations is a convention for reading, not a law of nature — we agree on it so an expression like 6 + 4 × 3 has exactly one meaning for everyone.

  • BEDMAS sets the order: Brackets, Exponents, Division/Multiplication (left to right), Addition/Subtraction (left to right). For example, in 6 + 4 × 3 the × is done first: 4 × 3 = 12, then 6 + 12 = 18.
  • × and ÷ rank equally, and + and − rank equally. When operations tie in rank, work strictly left to right. For example, 40 ÷ 5 × 2 = 8 × 2 = 16 (left to right), not 40 ÷ 10 = 4.
  • Brackets are always done first, no matter what operation is written inside them. For example, 2 × (3 + 4) = 2 × 7 = 14, whereas 2 × 3 + 4 is only 10.
  • Spreadsheets, calculators and computer code all follow BEDMAS exactly — get the order wrong in one formula and every total after it is wrong too. For example, the spreadsheet formula =2+3×4 returns 14 (the × first), not 20.
The orderBrackets → Exponents → ×, ÷ (left to right) → +, − (left to right)
🥤 Splitting a restaurant bill

3 mains at $12 each, plus a $6 shared dessert: 3 × 12 + 6 = 36 + 6 = $42.

Not 3 × (12 + 6) = $54 — the multiplication happens before the addition unless brackets say otherwise.

📱 Phone data plan

A plan costs $20 base plus $5 per extra GB. For 3 extra GB: 20 + 5 × 3 = 20 + 15 = $35. The 5 × 3 is worked out before the $20 is added on.

🔢 A spreadsheet formula

A cell contains =10+2*5. It shows 20, not 60 — computers follow BEDMAS exactly, doing 2×5 first, then adding 10.

Why: multiplication is repeated addition, so it's 'stronger' and comes first. Without an agreed order, 6 + 4 × 3 could mean 30 or 18.
⚠ Watch outBEDMAS does not mean all division before all multiplication. They rank equally, so 40 ÷ 5 × 2 = 16, not 4 — work left to right.

  5. Add: 6 + 25 = 31.

🕵️ Mistake detective

Diagnose the misconception, then reveal the cause and intervention.

8 − 2 × 3 = 18 (they did 8 − 2, then × 3).
Cause

They went left-to-right, but × comes before −.

Diagnostic question

What rule or representation would show why this answer cannot be right?

Intervention

Do 2 × 3 = 6 first, then 8 − 6 = 2.

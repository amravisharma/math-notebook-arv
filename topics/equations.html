👀 Notice & wonder

Before any rules: just look. What do you notice? What do you wonder?

A balance scale sits perfectly level: a sealed bag plus 4 identical coins on the left, 10 of the same coins on the right.

You might notice…
  • Level pans mean the two sides weigh the same
  • The bag’s weight is unknown but fixed
You might wonder…
  • How many coins’ worth is inside the bag?
  • Which moves could you make without tipping the scale?

Hold on to those wonderings — this chapter answers most of them.

📖 Read the chapter

An equation says two expressions are equal. Solving finds the unknown that keeps the balance true — by doing the same to both sides.

An equation asserts that two expressions name the same number. Picture a balance scale: the two sides weigh the same, and your job is to isolate the unknown without ever tipping it.

  • Solving means finding the value of the unknown that keeps both sides equal.
  • Every step must be a "legal move": add, subtract, multiply or divide both sides equally so the balance stays true.
  • To undo an operation, apply its opposite (its inverse) — undo in the reverse order to how the expression was built.
  • Always check your answer by substituting it back into the original equation — both sides should come out equal.
  • If the unknown appears on both sides (like 3x + 5 = x + 13), do one extra move first: subtract the smaller x-term from both sides to gather all the x's on one side, then solve as usual. Subtract x from both sides → 2x + 5 = 13 → 2x = 8 → x = 4. The balance idea is unchanged — you are just lifting an x-shaped weight off each pan.

💳 Saving for a skateboard

You have $35 saved and want a $95 skateboard, saving $12 a week: 35 + 12w = 95 → 12w = 60 → w = 5 weeks.

🌡️ Temperature conversion

F = 1.8C + 32. If today is 68°F: 1.8C + 32 = 68 → 1.8C = 36 → C = 20°C.

🎥 Movie tickets

A group booking costs an $8 fee plus $14 per ticket. If the total was $92: 8 + 14t = 92 → 14t = 84 → t = 6 tickets.

Why 'same to both sides'? An equation is a balanced scale. Whatever you do to one side you must do to the other, undoing operations in reverse to free the unknown.
⚠ Watch outWhatever you do, do it to both whole sides. And moving a term across the equals sign flips its sign — it doesn’t keep it.
⚡ Mental maths strategyUndo in reverse order, like taking off shoes before socks: if the equation was built by “×3 then +5”, solve by “−5 then ÷3”.

🕵️ Mistake detective

Diagnose the misconception, then reveal the cause and intervention.

2x = 10 → x = 8 (they subtracted 2).
Cause

x is multiplied by 2, so undo it with division, not subtraction.

Diagnostic question

What rule or representation would show why this answer cannot be right?

Intervention

Divide both sides by 2: x = 10 ÷ 2 = 5.

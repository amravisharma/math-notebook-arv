🔮 Discover — predict first

There are 100 cm in a metre. So how many centimetres in 2.5 m — and do you multiply or divide?

250 cm. A smaller unit means more of them, so multiply: 2.5 × 100 = 250.

📖 Read the chapter

Metric units scale by powers of 10 (1 km = 1000 m). Compound measures like speed combine two quantities (distance ÷ time), and one triangle relationship finds any of the three.

The metric system runs on powers of ten: prefixes like kilo-, centi- and milli- just slide the decimal point.

  • 1 km = 1000 m, 1 m = 100 cm — converting to a smaller unit gives a bigger number.
  • A rate combines two different kinds of unit, like speed (distance per time) or a price per kilogram.
  • speed = distance ÷ time, which rearranges to distance = speed × time, or time = distance ÷ speed.
  • Always make units match before calculating — mixing minutes with hours, or cm with m, is the classic slip.
  • Speed itself has two common "outfits": km/h and m/s. To go from km/h to m/s, divide by 3.6 (since 1000 m ÷ 3600 s = 1 ÷ 3.6) — so 90 km/h = 90 ÷ 3.6 = 25 m/s.

🍳 Following a recipe

A recipe needs 250 ml of milk, but your jug is marked in litres. Since 1 L = 1000 ml, 250 ml = 0.25 L — converting first avoids pouring the wrong amount.

✈️ Planning a road trip

A trip is 360 km at 90 km/h. Time = distance ÷ speed = 360 ÷ 90 = 4 hours — but only once both are already in matching units.

💊 Reading a medicine dose

A label says 5 mg per kg of body weight. For a 20 kg child, that's 5 × 20 = 100 mg — mixing up mg and g here could mean a dose 1000 times too strong.

🚴 A cyclist's mixed-unit speed

A cyclist rides 24 km in 30 minutes. 30 minutes is 0.5 hours — convert the time first: speed = 24 ÷ 0.5 = 48 km/h. Dividing 24 by "30" (forgetting the unit switch) gives a wrong, much smaller answer.

Why speed = distance ÷ time? It's 'distance per one unit of time'. Rearranging gives distance = speed × time and time = distance ÷ speed.
⚠ Watch outConverting to a smaller unit gives a bigger number. And always match units (hours with hours) before you calculate.
⚡ Mental maths strategyConverting to a smaller unit? Expect a bigger number. Metres to centimetres must give more centimetres (×100), never fewer.

🕵️ Mistake detective

Diagnose the misconception, then reveal the cause and intervention.

2.5 m = 25 cm.
Cause

They multiplied by 10 instead of 100 (there are 100 cm in a metre).

Diagnostic question

What rule or representation would show why this answer cannot be right?

Intervention

2.5 × 100 = 250 cm.

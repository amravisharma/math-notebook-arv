🔮 Discover — predict first

There are 100 cm in a metre. So how many centimetres in 2.5 m — and do you multiply or divide?

250 cm. A smaller unit means more of them, so multiply: 2.5 × 100 = 250.

📖 Read the chapter

Metric units scale by powers of 10 (1 km = 1000 m). Compound measures like speed combine two quantities (distance ÷ time), and one triangle relationship finds any of the three.

The metric system runs on powers of ten: the prefixes kilo-, centi-, milli- just slide the decimal point. A rate links two different units, and the most common is speed.

Speed relationshipspeed = distance ÷ time (rearranges three ways)

The same triangle gives distance = speed × time and time = distance ÷ speed. The golden habit is to make units match before calculating — mixing minutes with hours, or cm with m, is the classic slip.

Why speed = distance ÷ time? It's 'distance per one unit of time'. Rearranging gives distance = speed × time and time = distance ÷ speed.
⚠ Watch outConverting to a smaller unit gives a bigger number. And always match units (hours with hours) before you calculate.

MediumMixed method
A car travels 150 km in 2 h. Find its average speed.
  1. Speed = distance ÷ time.
  2. = 150 ÷ 2 = 75 km/h.
Answer75 km/h
HardChallenge scenario
A cyclist rides 45 km in 1 h 30 min. Speed, then time for 100 km?
  1. 1 h 30 min = 1.5 h → 45 ÷ 1.5 = 30 km/h.
  2. 100 ÷ 30 ≈ 3.33 h = 3 h 20 min.
Answer30 km/h; 3 h 20 min

🕵️ Mistake detective

Diagnose the misconception, then reveal the cause and intervention.

2.5 m = 25 cm.
Cause

They multiplied by 10 instead of 100 (there are 100 cm in a metre).

Diagnostic question

What rule or representation would show why this answer cannot be right?

Intervention

2.5 × 100 = 250 cm.

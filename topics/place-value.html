🔮 Discover — predict first

Take 5 and keep sliding it one place left: 5, 50, 500, 5000. What happens to its value at each step?

It becomes ten times bigger every step. That one rule — ×10 per place to the left — is the whole reason our number system works.

📖 Read the chapter

Every digit has a value that depends on its position. Moving one place left multiplies its value by 10 — the single rule our whole number system is built on.

Our number system is base ten and positional: a numeral's value is the sum of each digit multiplied by the value of its place.

  • Each digit's value depends on where it sits. Moving one place left multiplies that digit's value by 10. For example, the 4 in 4073 is worth 4000, but the 4 in 40 is worth only 40.
  • A number's total value is the sum of digit × place value (its expanded form): 4073 = 4×1000 + 0×100 + 7×10 + 3.
  • Zero is not "nothing" — it is a placeholder that holds a column open so the other digits keep their correct value. For example, in 405 the 0 keeps the 4 in the hundreds column; drop it and 405 becomes just 45.
  • To round, look only at the digit just past the place you are keeping: 5 or more rounds up, anything less stays the same. For example, 374 to the nearest 10 → the ones digit 4 rounds down to 370, but 376 → 380.
Definitionvalue = Σ (digit × place value)
each place is 10× the one on its right
💵 Reading a price tag

$1,000 and $100 look almost alike on a receipt — but one extra zero makes the first price ten times as much money.

A cash register, a bank app and a price tag all depend on place value to keep prices honest.

🚗 Car odometer

A car's odometer reads 45,000 km. After a 10 km trip it reads 45,010 km.

Only the tens and ones digits changed — the ten-thousands, thousands and hundreds digits kept their value untouched.

🌎 A city's population

A town of 84,000 people grows to 840,000. That is not "a couple more digits" — it is exactly ten times bigger, because every digit shifted one place to the left.

Why it works: we group in tens, so the same digit is worth ten times more each step to the left. The position does the work, not the digit itself.
⚠ Watch outA zero isn't “nothing” — it holds a column open. Drop it from 405 and you get 45, a completely different number.
⚡ Mental maths strategyTo multiply by 10, don’t “add a zero” — shift every digit one place left. It works for decimals too: 3.5 × 10 = 35, not 3.50.

🕵️ Mistake detective

Diagnose the misconception, then reveal the cause and intervention.

In 3452, the digit 4 is worth 4.
Cause

The 4 sits in the hundreds column, so its position matters.

Diagnostic question

What rule or representation would show why this answer cannot be right?

Intervention

4 in the hundreds place is worth 4 × 100 = 400.

🌍 In the real world

Place value is why $1,000 and $100 look almost alike but aren't — one extra zero, ten times the money. Odometers, bank apps and cash registers all depend on it.

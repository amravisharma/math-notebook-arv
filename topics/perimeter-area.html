🔮 Discover — predict first

A rectangle is 5 cm by 3 cm. Count the squares inside, then walk around the edge. Which count needs multiplying and which needs adding?

Inside (area) = 5 × 3 = 15 squares. Around (perimeter) = 5 + 3 + 5 + 3 = 16 cm. Area multiplies; perimeter adds.

📖 Read the chapter

Perimeter is the distance around a shape (add the sides). Area is the space inside (in squares). Compound shapes split into rectangles and triangles.

Perimeter is a one-dimensional length around the edge of a shape; area is a two-dimensional covering, counted in unit squares.

  • Key area formulas: rectangle = length × width; triangle = ½ × base × height (exactly half its surrounding rectangle); parallelogram = base × height. For example, a triangle with base 8 cm and height 5 cm has area ½ × 8 × 5 = 20 cm².
  • A trapezium has one pair of parallel sides (lengths a and b, a distance h apart). Its area is ½(a + b) × h — average the two parallel sides, then multiply by the height. For a = 6, b = 10, h = 4: ½(6 + 10) × 4 = ½ × 16 × 4 = 32.
  • For a triangle, "height" always means the perpendicular height — measured straight up from the base at a right angle — never the slanted side, even when the slanted side looks like the obvious measurement to use. For example, a triangle with base 6 cm and perpendicular height 4 cm has area ½ × 6 × 4 = 12 cm².
  • A compound shape (made of simpler shapes joined together) is found by splitting it into rectangles and triangles, then adding or subtracting their areas. For example, an L-shape splits into a 6 × 4 and a 3 × 2 rectangle → 24 + 6 = 30 units².
  • Perimeter and area measure genuinely different things — a shape can have a bigger perimeter but a smaller area than another shape. For example, a 1 × 8 rectangle has perimeter 18 but area 8, while a 4 × 4 square has a smaller perimeter (16) yet a bigger area (16).

triangle = ½ × b × h
parallelogram = b × h
trapezium = ½(a + b) × h
🚧 Fencing a garden

A rectangular garden is 8 m by 5 m. Fencing needs the perimeter: 2 × (8+5) = 26 m of fence.

🧹 Carpeting a room

A room is 4 m by 3.5 m. Carpet needed is the area: 4 × 3.5 = 14 m² — carpet is bought by area, not by the length of the walls.

📌 Two paddocks, same fence

A 10 m × 10 m paddock has perimeter 40 m and area 100 m². An 18 m × 2 m paddock also has perimeter 40 m, but only 36 m² — the same fencing can enclose very different areas.

Why length × width? You're counting unit squares that tile the shape. A triangle is half its surrounding rectangle, hence ½ × base × height.
⚠ Watch outPerimeter and area are different measures. A shape with a bigger perimeter can have a smaller area.
⚡ Mental maths strategyRectangle perimeter in your head: add length and width once, then double. 8 by 5 → 13, doubled → 26.

🕵️ Mistake detective

Diagnose the misconception, then reveal the cause and intervention.

Area of a 5 × 3 rectangle = 16 cm².
Cause

That's the perimeter (adding the sides), not the area.

Diagnostic question

What rule or representation would show why this answer cannot be right?

Intervention

Area = length × width = 5 × 3 = 15 cm².

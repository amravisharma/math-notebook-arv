🔮 Discover — predict first

A sheet of paper doubles in layers each time you fold it: 2, 4, 8… How many layers after 10 folds?

2¹⁰ = 1024 layers. Repeated doubling is a power, and powers grow astonishingly fast.

📚 Key words
base
the number being multiplied (the 2 in 2³)
index / exponent
how many times to multiply (the 3 in 2³)
square root
the number that was squared to give this value
scientific notation
a number as (1–10) × a power of 10
📖 Read the chapter

An index is repeated multiplication: 2³ = 2×2×2. A square root undoes squaring. Scientific notation writes very large/small numbers as a value between 1 and 10 times a power of 10.

An exponent (or index) counts how many times the base is multiplied by itself: 2³ = 2×2×2 (which is not 2×3).

  • Index law: when multiplying powers with the same base, add the exponents, never multiply them — aⁿ × aᴸ = aⁿ⁺ᴸ. So 2³ × 2² = 2⁵ = 32, not 2⁶ = 64.
  • A square root undoes squaring — it asks "what number, multiplied by itself, gives this value?" For example, √49 = 7, because 7 × 7 = 49.
  • Any base to the power 0 equals 1 (a⁰ = 1). See why by dividing down: 2³ = 8, 2² = 4, 2¹ = 2 — each step halves, so 2⁰ = 2 ÷ 2 = 1.
  • Scientific notation writes very large or very small numbers compactly, as a value between 1 and 10 times a power of 10. Large numbers use a positive power (150,000,000 = 1.5 × 10⁸); numbers smaller than 1 use a negative power — 0.0045 = 4.5 × 10⁻³ (the point moves 3 places right to make 4.5, so the exponent is −3). The leading digit is always 1–10, so every number has exactly one form.

🦠 Bacteria doubling

A bacteria colony doubles every hour, starting from 1 cell. After 10 hours there are 2¹⁰ = 1024 cells — powers describe repeated doubling far faster than adding.

💾 Phone storage

A phone has 128 GB of storage. 128 = 2⁷. Computer storage sizes (kilo, mega, giga) are built from powers of 2 and powers of 1000.

🌌 Distance to the Sun

The Sun is about 150,000,000 km from Earth. In scientific notation that's 1.5 × 10⁸ km — far easier to read and compare than counting zeros.

Why add indices when multiplying? 2³ × 2² = (2×2×2)×(2×2) = 2⁵ — you're just counting how many 2s are multiplied.
⚠ Watch out3² is 3 × 3 = 9, not 3 × 2 = 6. An exponent is repeated multiplication, not multiplication by the exponent.

🕵️ Mistake detective

Diagnose the misconception, then reveal the cause and intervention.

They wrote 2³ = 6 (2 × 3).
Cause

An index means repeated multiplication, not base × index.

Diagnostic question

What rule or representation would show why this answer cannot be right?

Intervention

2³ = 2 × 2 × 2 = 8.
